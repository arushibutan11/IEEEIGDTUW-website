IEEE WEEK

IEEE Week 2.0 (Day 1)- Break the Code

Date: 27th March, 2017
IEEE Week 2.0 started with the coding event Break the code. The event was conducted in association with Techsters, IGDTUW. The participants were given codes and were required to the numerical outputs and further convert these outputs to their corresponding ASCII Letters and form a word. It was a timed event. Over 20 teams participated in the event, each having two members. The event was highly appreciated by the participants.

IEEE Week 2.0 (Days 2, 3, and 4)- Letâ€™s Build: Smart Dustbin Workshop

Date(s): 28th-30th March, 2017
Students made Smart Bins using the Arduino platform, ultrasonic sensors and Bluetooth modules. The smart dustbins can be connected to a mobile app, which was made on MIT App Inventor 2 by the students. Once the dustbins are almost full (which is detected by the installed ultrasonic sensor), a message is sent to the connected mobile phone, where text is also converted into speech to create a notification. This workshop resulted in an environment - pro device, using recycling of plastic as well as paper/cardboard, with the help of electronic and programming skills.

IEEE Week 2.0 (Day 3)- Enigma

Date: 29th March, 2017
Enigma, a non-technical quiz, was organised on the third day of IEEE Week 2.0. It had questions ranging from pop-culture to general knowledge and the last question consisted of unjumbling select letters from the answers to previous questions. It was a well received and interesting event.
